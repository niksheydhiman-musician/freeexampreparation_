## Start with the exam mindset

NID DAT checks how you observe, think, communicate, and solve problems—not just how well you draw.

## Topic-wise approach

### 1. Observation and visual analysis

- Observe real objects and spaces daily.
- Practice converting observations into quick sketches.
- Train yourself to explain visual decisions in simple language.

### 2. Design principles

- Build clarity on balance, proportion, contrast, hierarchy, and rhythm.
- Solve short exercises where you apply one principle at a time.

### 3. Creativity and ideation

- Do 15-minute concept generation drills.
- Practice multiple solution routes before selecting one.

### 4. Storytelling and communication

- Learn to structure answers: context → idea → execution.
- Keep annotations neat and useful.

## Weekly routine

- 4 days concept practice
- 2 days timed question solving
- 1 day review and refinement
