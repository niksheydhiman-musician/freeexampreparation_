What's Actually Confirmed Right Now

Before anything else, here's the part that matters most. IIT Bombay has already made an official announcement about UCEED 2027, and it does not point to December 2026 for registration. Several pages online are circulating that month anyway. Worth correcting directly before you plan your year around it.

Confirmed: IIT Bombay will conduct UCEED 2027 and CEED 2027 on January 17, 2027, in a single morning session. This date has been officially announced. Detailed information brochures for both exams are expected on October 1, 2026.

Tentative, based on IIT Bombay's own schedule: Online registration is expected to begin in the first week of October 2026, with the regular deadline likely falling in the first week of November and a late fee window stretching into the second week of November. None of this is December.

So if you've seen "registration opens December 2026" anywhere, treat it as inaccurate based on the most recent official signal from IIT Bombay. The real window points to early October, not December, which is a meaningful two-month difference if you're planning your preparation schedule around it.

Why December 2026 Doesn't Match the Real Pattern

It's worth understanding where a December estimate might come from, since it doesn't match either IIT Bombay's recent history or its own fresh announcement for 2027.

UCEED registration has opened in either September or October for every recent cycle. UCEED 2022 registration opened on September 9, 2021. UCEED 2026 registration opened on October 1, 2025. Both sit firmly in the September to October range, not December. A December estimate likely comes from confusing UCEED's timeline with a different exam's cycle, since several other design entrance exams, including NIFT, have opened applications in that later window.

UCEED and NIFT run on genuinely different calendars. NIFT's exam falls in February, so its application window typically opens in the preceding November or December. UCEED's exam falls in January, a month earlier, which pulls its entire registration cycle forward into September or October. Mixing up these two timelines is an easy mistake, but it's worth keeping them separate when you're tracking actual deadlines.

Fee Structure and Who Can Apply

Based on recent cycles, expect a fee structure broadly similar to what UCEED 2027's own preliminary reporting suggests, with a reduced fee for female, Scheduled Caste, Scheduled Tribe, and Persons with Disabilities candidates, set at around two thousand rupees, and a higher standard fee for other categories. The exact figures, along with any changes, will only be confirmed once IIT Bombay releases the official brochure on October 1.

UCEED qualification is required for admission to the B.Des program at IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Guwahati, IIT Hyderabad, IIT Roorkee, and IIITDM Jabalpur. A single common application form, processed through the IIT Bombay Admissions Portal, covers all participating institutes, and seat allocation runs across five rounds based on All India Rank, category, and institute preference.

How to Be Ready Before October

With the exam date already fixed, you have a genuinely firm deadline to prepare around, even before registration opens. Use the next few months well.

  • Treat January 17, 2027 as your real target. The exam date is confirmed. Build your preparation calendar backward from that date rather than waiting for the registration window to feel like the starting gun.
  • Keep documents ready well before October. Photograph, signature, category certificate where applicable, and educational records in standard formats. Having these ready removes one entire source of last-minute stress once the portal opens.
  • Decide your institute preferences now. IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Guwahati, IIT Hyderabad, IIT Roorkee, and IIITDM Jabalpur each have different seat counts and campus character. Thinking this through early beats a rushed decision during the application window.
  • Practice Part A and Part B separately, then together. UCEED's computer-based Part A and pen-and-paper Part B test different skills. Build strength in each independently before combining them in full three-hour mock sessions.
  • Don't let date confusion eat into study time. Whether registration opens in the first or second week of October changes nothing about how much drawing, visualization, and reasoning practice you need between now and January.
