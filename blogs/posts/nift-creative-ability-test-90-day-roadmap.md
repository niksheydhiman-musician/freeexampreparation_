## Why a 90-day plan works

A focused 90-day schedule gives enough time to build creative fundamentals, improve speed, and revise important exam patterns without burnout.

## Phase 1 (Days 1–30): Foundation

- Understand NIFT CAT and GAT structure clearly.
- Start daily sketching and observation exercises.
- Build vocabulary, comprehension, and basic quantitative speed.
- Solve beginner-level design aptitude questions.

## Phase 2 (Days 31–60): Skill building

- Move to timed CAT question practice.
- Solve mixed GAT sets in fixed slots.
- Start weekly full-length mock sessions.
- Track mistakes in a simple error log.

## Phase 3 (Days 61–90): Exam simulation

- Solve full mocks in realistic test conditions.
- Focus on weak topics from your error log.
- Improve presentation, layout clarity, and answer quality.
- Follow a final 10-day light revision strategy.

## Final checklist

- Daily 2–3 hours minimum focused study.
- Weekly mock and review.
- Dedicated revision notes for repeated mistakes.
- Proper sleep and exam-day routine practice.
