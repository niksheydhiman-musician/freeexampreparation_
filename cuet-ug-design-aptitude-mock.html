📋 CUET Fine Arts Syllabus & Pattern Summary

This module includes key conceptual and historical questions testing your knowledge of traditional schools, modern Indian movements, and colonial documentation dynamics.

Q1 History • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

For what purpose did British colonial officers commission local artists to paint scenes around them?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (B) For documentation

Before photography was invented, painting was the most effective medium for documenting visual surroundings. Local Indian artists trained in traditional miniature styles were commissioned to create highly detailed and accurate representations.

Q2 Collections • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

Where is the artwork 'Babur Inspecting the Fort of Gwalior' by Bhure currently displayed?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (D) National Museum, New Delhi

This painting is part of the illustrated Baburnama manuscript from the 16th century. The National Museum at New Delhi maintains this extensive imperial collection.

Q3 Mughal School • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

Which two master artists came to India after Humayun regained power?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (C) Mir Sayyid Ali and Abd us Samad

They brought sophisticated techniques from Safavid Persia and blended them with indigenous Indian styles, laying down the actual foundation of the iconic Mughal School workshop.

Q4 Movements • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

Which art styles inspired Abanindranath Tagore and E.B. Havell?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (C) Mughal and Pahari Miniature

The Bengal School founders rejected Western academic approaches. They drew design inspiration from local heritage, particularly the graceful fluid dynamics of Mughal and Pahari miniature traditions alongside Ajanta murals.

Q5 Themes • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

What were the primary themes in Raja Ravi Varma's paintings?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (B) Ramayana and Mahabharata

Raja Ravi Varma fused classic European academic oil painting oil rendering with traditional Indian literary themes, mass-producing oleographs that reached households throughout India.

Q6 Institutions • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

Who founded the Indian Society of Oriental Art?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (C) Abanindranath Tagore

Established in Calcutta in 1907, Abanindranath Tagore was the primary ideological lead and mentor, promoting localized indigenous forms over Western styles.

Q7 Nationalism • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

Which artist painted the Haripura posters?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (B) Nandalal Bose

Commissioned by Mahatma Gandhi for the 1938 Indian National Congress session at Haripura, these handmade posters beautifully depicted local agrarian rural life dynamics.

Q8 Aesthetics • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

What were the primary characteristics of artworks produced under Shah Jahan's patronage?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (A) Subliminal qualities and exalted beautification

Art shifted to highly formal, opulent, and heavily idealized visual styles featuring high technical layout precision and intense gold leaf application.

Q9 Deccani School • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

To which school of miniature painting does the painting 'Yogini' belong?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (D) The Deccani School of Miniature Painting

Characterized by elongated figures and deep mystical settings, this female ascetic artwork is specifically attributed to the Bijapur sub-school of the Deccan.

Q10 Technique • 1 Mark 📄 CUET 2025

What technique did Abdul Rehman Chughtai use in his works?

💡 View Solution

✓ Answer: (A) Wash technique

Associated with the Bengal School, this involved layered thin translucent pigment mixed with frequent water baths to create an ethereal, signature atmosphere.
